Privacy & Security

How we handle data as an AI-first CRM. Last updated: November 3, 2025

Note: This is a practical template and not legal advice. Review with counsel for your use case.

Roles & scope

For customer lead and contact data stored in Rithink, we act as a processor. For our own website visitors and marketing, we act as a controller.

What we process

  • Contact and company details (e.g., name, email, role)
  • Interactions (SMS threads, bookings, summaries, notes)
  • Operational metadata (owners, SLAs, cadence steps, timestamps)

Purposes

  • To capture and respond to inbound leads and book meetings
  • To operate CRM records, pipelines, tasks and messaging sequences
  • To measure and improve response times and conversion

Legal basis (GDPR)

  • Legitimate interests in responding to business inquiries and operating CRM workflows
  • Consent where required (e.g., subscriptions, certain cookies)
  • Contract for customers using our product

Security

  • Encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest
  • Role-based access controls, 2FA/SSO options
  • Backups and disaster recovery procedures
  • Least-privilege policies

AI & model usage

  • Customer data is not used to train foundation models by default.
  • Tenant-level fine-tuning or prompt libraries only with explicit opt-in.
  • We retain operational prompts/summaries as part of your CRM records.

Retention

CRM records persist while your account is active. You can export or delete data on request; backups roll off on a schedule.

Sub-processors & transfers

We use reputable providers for hosting, messaging and analytics. Where data leaves the EEA, we use appropriate safeguards (e.g., SCCs). A current sub-processor list is available on request.

Data subject rights

  • Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection (where applicable)

  • DPA: available on request

Cookies

We use essential cookies and may use analytics with consent. You can change preferences at any time.
